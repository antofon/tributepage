Dr. Mae C. Jemison

Dr. Mae C. Jemison was born October 17, 1956 in Decatur, Alabama. She grew up in Alabama, yet was raised in Chicago where she enjoyed dancing as a young child. At age 16 she earned a scholarship to attend Stanford University, where she double majored in Chemical Engineering and African/Afro-American Studies.

Upon her graduation from Stanford University, she went on to attend medical school at Cornell University. (Fun Fact: She initially had dreams of becoming a professional dancer). She gained more experience in the medical field working as a General Practitioner in LA and as a Peace Corps Medical Officer in West Africa.

In June 1987, she was selected for the NASA astronaut program. After her rigorous training was complete, in 1992, she became the first African American woman to travel into space. She served as a co-investigator for bone cell research on 8-day mission aboard the Space Shuttle Endeavor.
